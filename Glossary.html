Here are some key terms you might encounter when studying the periodic table:

Atomic Number (Z):
The number of protons present in an atom's nucleus. It defines the chemical identity of an element.
Atomic Mass (A):
The average mass of an element's atoms, calculated based on the abundance of its natural isotopes. It is expressed in atomic mass units (u).
Isotope:
Atoms of the same element (thus having the same atomic number) that have a different number of neutrons in the nucleus, and consequently a different atomic mass.
Group:
A vertical column in the periodic table. Elements in the same group tend to have similar chemical properties.
Period:
A horizontal row in the periodic table. Elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells.
Electronegativity:
The tendency of an atom to attract electrons to itself when forming a chemical bond with another atom.
Ion:
An atom or molecule that has gained or lost one or more electrons, and thus has a net electrical charge (positive or negative).
