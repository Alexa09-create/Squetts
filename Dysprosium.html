Abundance in Earth's Crust:
Approximately 6.2 mg/kg, classified as "moderately abundant" among rare earths.
Characteristics:
  • Rare earth metal.
  • Bright silvery color.
  • Good resistance to oxidation at room temperature, but oxidizes easily when heated.
  • It is paramagnetic at room temperature and ferromagnetic below -188 ¬∞C.
  • Reactive with water and acids.
Technological Applications:
  • High-performance permanent magnets (together with neodymium).
  • Lasers and optical components.
  • Components for nuclear reactors (absorbs neutrons).
  • Electronic devices (hard drives, headphones, speakers).
  • Halogen vapor lamps and lanterns.
  • Additive in alloys to improve thermal resistance.
Main Deposit Locations:
  • China (approx. 80% of world production).
  • Australia, India, Russia, Brazil, USA.
  • It is mainly extracted from minerals such as monazite and bastnasite.
Health Effects:
  • Metallic dysprosium is generally non-toxic in low concentrations.
  • Its compounds can be irritating to eyes, skin, and mucous membranes.
  • Chronic exposure to rare earth dusts can cause respiratory problems.
Environmental Effects:
  • Low environmental risk if used correctly.
  • However, extraction and refining can produce radioactive waste (due to the presence of thorium).
  • It can contaminate soil and water if improperly disposed of.
