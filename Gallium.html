Gallium

Gallium is a very pure metal with a wonderful silvery color

What is Gallium?

Solid gallium is a blue-gray metal with an orthorhombic crystal structure, and its atomic number is 31.

Uses in electronic devices

  • Powerful Magnets: High-speed integrated circuits.
  • Displays: Radio-frequency power amplifiers.
  • Sensors and Circuits: Laser diodes and infrared LEDs.

Environmental Impact

  • Pollution: Gallium is not found in its own deposits, but is extracted as a byproduct during the processing of other minerals.
  • Toxicity: This extraction process can contribute to water pollution and soil degradation, similar to the general environmental impacts of aluminum and zinc extraction.
  • Toxicity: Gallium production also involves emissions of harmful substances, potentially contributing to climate change.

Physico-chemical characteristics

  • Atomic number: 31
  • Atomic mass: 69.72 g.mol-1
