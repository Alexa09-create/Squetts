Abundance:
Europium is one of the least abundant rare earth elements, nearly as abundant as tin
Characteristics:
  • A soft silvery metal, both rare and expensive
  • Part of the lanthanide group
  • Forms oxide layers when exposed to oxygen
  • The most reactive element in the lanthanide group
Technological Applications:
  • Screens and Displays: Used in phosphors for color displays and LEDs
  • Lasers and Optical Materials
  • Used in control rods and radiation shields in nuclear reactors
  • Found in fluorescent inks used in banknotes
Location of Deposits:
  • China and the United States, where approximately 100 tons are produced annually
Health Effects:
Europium salts may be slightly toxic if ingested, but their toxicity has not been fully investigated
Environmental Effects:
Europium does not pose a threat to plants or animals, but metal dust can present an explosion hazard
